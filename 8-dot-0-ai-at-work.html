Presenter on screen:

"Welcome to Topic 8: AI at Work. This week, we're going to dive deep into how to bring AI projects to life and what makes a successful use of AI.

Cut to visuals of people on a computer coding.

When we think of AI, we often think of techies. Coders, mathematicians and engineers. It's a problem for the tech team right?

Once you are in the workplace, you'll quickly learn that big projects have many different stakeholders. Sometimes you are just trying to solve a problem. It is becoming more and more common that some new start up will claim they can use AI to solve all of your problems. AI can often be a useful tool for solving problems, but it is only part of the picture. Understanding what problems exist, what technologies exist and how the two can work together is key to any successful AI enterprise.

It is all to common to hear phrases like "we could use AI" without any real thought being given to whether it is the right tool for the job. We will investigate a few example case studies to tease out the important questions in any AI project. What data do we need? What data do we have? What tools do we have access to and how are we going to deploy the system?

Don't worry, we won't expect you to start training your own Large Language Models or inventing the newest AI tools, rather we will be consider AI from the perspective of the outsider. The user, the consumer, the service provider. By the end of this week, you should have a good grasp of the main things you need to know about any AI project.
