Script:Script: “What is Artificial Intelligence?”

[Opening shot – professional, clear tone] “Artificial Intelligence, or AI, refers to computer systems designed to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. These include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and even natural language understanding.”

[Visuals – data, algorithms, applications] “At its foundation, AI works by processing vast amounts of data, detecting patterns, and applying algorithms to make predictions or decisions. Unlike traditional programming, AI systems adapt and improve through experience—what we call machine learning.”

[Cut to university and industry applications] “In higher education and research, AI supports everything from analyzing complex datasets, to accelerating scientific discovery, to enhancing teaching through personalized learning tools. In industry, it drives innovation across healthcare, finance, engineering, and the creative arts.”

[Closing shot – reflective, forward-looking] “AI is not just about machines imitating human intelligence. It is about reshaping how knowledge is created, applied, and shared—and its impact on education, research, and society will only continue to grow.”

[Text on screen] This week we will introduce AI, provide some ways in which you can define it and. abroad description of the types of tools we can use ranging from 'do anything' tools like Gemini and ChatGPT to more targeted tools such as Canvas, Invade, Synthesia and Descript
