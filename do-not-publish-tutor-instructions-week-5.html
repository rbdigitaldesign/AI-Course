Activity Descriptions

Task #1: catch-up

Not all students will have prepared. This activity serves to integrate the prepared and non-prepared students.

  1. Ask students to honestly show their hand if they have not prepared for this workshop (there is no punishment nor repercussion to this: it will just help the tutor frame the learning better)
  2. Ask the students who have not prepared, to independently read 5.1 topic on Canvas [$WIKI_REFERENCE$/pages/g56b595438287b29126cc4dfb7769005b?module_item_id=gfa276aeca32d8fa3b450f3fabdcd2344]
  3. Ask students to visit Moltbook on their device [https://www.moltbook.com/m/general]
  4. Explain that Moltbook is a website that hosts online chats on topics about AI. Importantly, only AI agents can contribute to the chats. Most of the AI agents contributing in there have been created by people and then imported into the chat space (though some AI agents have actually been coded within the website by other AI agents].
  • In pairs or table groups, choose one chat and scroll to read the comment thread. Remember that all the comments are being entered by an AI agent, so without direct human input.
  • Search & Find Task: In pairs or table groups, choose one chat and scroll to read the comment thread. Look for any of these elements:
    * repeated content
    * consensus / overall agreement on a viewpoint
    * changed viewpoints over time
    * agents helping other agents function better
  • Deeper questions to consider:
    * At face value how do these comment threads differ from human comments threads?
    * Who holds agency in this website?

Task #2: Table discussion

Bring the whole class together.

  • Ask for some observations and analysis on Moltbook to be shared with the whole class.
  • Now focus on the case studies that students read in preparation. In groups, students will discuss the case studies they read for this week. There are some prompting questions.

 

Task #3: Footprint Calculator

Perhaps use a google sheet to collate numbers students have completed to get a sense of AI usage in the class? They should use the Carbon Calculator to do so.

Prompting Questions:

  • As a class, what is our impact?
  • How has/will this course affected your use of AI (more/less/same)?
  • Is there anything we can/could do to help offset these impacts?

Task #3: Structured Debate

  • Tables are split into for AI and against AI and given time to prep and share pre-class research.
    • For Pro-AI Groups: How does AI contribute positively to the sectors you researched (the diversity of research in the group should help)? What are the most compelling examples of AI's benefits from the case studies and your prep activity? How can the risks associated with AI be mitigated effectively?
    • For Anti-AI Groups: What are the significant negative impacts of AI in this sectors? How do the examples from the case studies and research illustrate the potential dangers of AI? What ethical dilemmas arise from the use of AI, and why might they outweigh the benefits?

  • Reflection and Takeaways

    • Whole Class Post-Debate Discussion: Facilitate a reflective conversation on what students learned through the debate process.
