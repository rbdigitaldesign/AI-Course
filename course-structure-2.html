How to take this course

Welcome to artificial intelligence. This course does not follow the usual “content in, test out” pattern. Instead, you will build your understanding by exploring ideas, testing tools, and reflecting on what you learn as you go.

Across the course, we will explore questions such as:

  • What can artificial intelligence do?
  • How do we define artificial intelligence?
  • Which tools can we use, and what are they suited to?

To show your learning over time, you will create an ePortfolio. This is where you collect your work and reflections as evidence of your progress.

Assessment: What is an ePortfolio?

Your ePortfolio is not a checklist. Two ePortfolios should look different because they reflect different interests, approaches, and learning journeys. The goal is to show your thinking and development over time.

A definition

An ePortfolio is a digital collection of a person’s work, reflections, and achievements that demonstrates their skills, learning, and experiences over time. During this course, we will provide prompts, activities, and ideas, and many of these can become part of your ePortfolio.

Your ePortfolio might include:

  • Projects, essays, code, artwork, reports, designs, calculations, and other artefacts.
  • Reflections, including what you did and did not know at the start, how you approached problems, and what changed.
  • An overall theme or emphasis that ties your work together.

How it will be assessed

You might be thinking:

  • “Each week I post the activity, write a reflection, and I’m done.”

Assessment is broader than that. Your ePortfolio will be reviewed as a whole, based on how well it demonstrates the course learning outcomes and Adelaide University’s graduate qualities.

The relevant graduate qualities for this course are:

  • Ethical leader
  • Strategic problem solver
  • Resilient thinker
  • Trusted communicator

As you build your ePortfolio, use the graduate qualities and the course learning outcomes as a lens.

Ask yourself: "Does my work and reflection provide evidence of these?" That is what assessors will look for.

Logistics: Online learning

We will provide readings, videos, and activities, but these are guides rather than limits. You are expected to look beyond the course pages and follow your curiosity, using the provided materials as a starting point.

Commons workshop

Once per week, you will attend a 2-hour workshop (face-to-face or online, no mixed classes) with 20 to 50 students and facilitators. These sessions are not traditional tutorials with pre-prepared answers. They are structured opportunities to discuss your ideas, test thinking, and learn from others.

The typical structure will be:

  • Summary of the weekly topic (10 minutes)
  • Class discussion and tutor feedback on weekly tasks (10 minutes)
  • Primary class activity (40 minutes)
  • Class discussion and facilitator feedback on outcomes (20 minutes)
  • Secondary class activity (30 minutes)
    • Including an opportunity to add to your ePortfolio
  • Wrap-up and feedback (10 minutes)
