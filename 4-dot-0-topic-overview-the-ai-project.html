This week marks a shift in focus from learning about AI to applying your knowledge in a collaborative, creative and critical way. You’ll work in small groups to design and plan a project that explores how AI can be used to address a real-world problem. This is your opportunity to be brave, stretch your thinking, and challenge yourselves to use AI meaningfully and responsibly.

Your project doesn’t need to solve the problem; it’s about the journey: understanding what AI is, how it can be used, and what responsible use looks like in different disciplines.

Draft Script for Intro Video

Presenter on screen:

"Welcome to Topic 4: The AI Project. This week, we’re shifting gears. You’ve spent the last few topics learning what AI is, how it works, and how it’s used in education and research. Now, it’s time to apply that knowledge in a real-world context.

This topic is all about setting up your group project. You’ll work with your peers to explore a problem, think critically about how AI might be involved, and begin planning your approach. It’s not about solving the problem, it’s about diagnosing it, understanding what’s possible, and identifying what you’ll need to explore further."

Cut to visuals of students in discussion, sticky notes, digital collaboration tools.

"You’ll be challenged to think creatively and strategically. What kind of problem interests you? Is it something in your discipline, your community, or the wider world? Could AI help make board games more engaging? Could it improve travel safety? Could it support mental health, sustainability, or accessibility?

You’ll choose from a list of over 100 possible project ideas or propose your own. The goal isn’t to find the perfect answer, but to use AI thoughtfully to explore the problem space."

Cut to visuals of AI tools in use: Gemini, Perplexity, ChatGPT, design software.

"Your project might be: Creative – designing a solution using AI tools. Investigative – using AI to gather and analyse information. Explorative – imagining how AI could be used in the future. Whatever direction you take, your group will need to: Define the problem clearly. Plan how you’ll use AI tools. Assign roles and responsibilities. Begin researching and gathering insights."
Cut to visuals of students using Microsoft Teams, planning boards, and ePortfolio tools.

"You’ll also start building your individual ePortfolio, where you’ll reflect on your role, your learning, and how AI is shaping your thinking. This is your space to document the journey, not just the outcome."

Presenter back on screen:

"Remember, this week is about planning, not presenting. Be bold. Ask questions. Think critically. And most importantly, work together."

"AI is changing the way we approach problems. This project is your chance to explore that change, apply your knowledge, and stretch your thinking. Let’s get started."

By the end of this topic, you will know:

  • Collaboratively plan and scope a project using AI tools.
  • Apply your understanding of AI to a real-world context.
  • Reflect on the ethical and practical implications of AI use for your project
