Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is revolutionising the way we work, learn and interact with technology. This course will introduce AI as the most influential technology to be introduced in the last 30 years and will both encourage and support students in developing a comprehensive understanding of how AI is transforming learning, influencing changes in industry and shaping societal dynamics.

As AI becomes more ingrained in daily life, it raises fundamental questions about ethics, bias, privacy and social responsibility. Through debates, discussions, and ethical case studies, students will critically analyse AI’s societal impact, evaluate ethical concerns, and explore solutions to ensure AI serves humanity responsibly. Students will reflect upon presentations from high-profile speakers in inter-disciplinary workshops to understand the use of AI in the world and its impact, what ‘responsible use of AI’ means, and the ethical dilemmas of AI in decision-making and social justice. Students will document their progress weekly in a portfolio and choose AI projects based on their interests. They will be supported by regular feedback as they progress through the course, moving from demystifying AI, understanding the breadth and challenges in its use, relevant legal and ethical ramifications, and finally put their understanding into practice through a project. Students will summarise their learnings in a report built from their portfolio.

Course Aims and Learning Outcomes

Students will discover AI’s potential benefits and risks and will explore the technical, ethical and legal issues related to AI, while developing expertise and understanding for how to responsibly apply AI in their studies, workplace and society. Students will work in an interdisciplinary, collaborative environment to demystify AI and develop meaningful expertise in its use through flexible and personalised assessment. The course will encourage collaboration, with a goal towards developing a culture of learning assisted by technology and multiple human perspectives.

Assessment Overview

The course will be functionally assessed as a single large ePortfolio of work, comprising of multiple smaller components. The smaller components will be assessed against a qualitative rubric, with feedback provided at several junctures in the course. The feedback will indicate whether the student is ‘on track’ to achieving the standard, or not, or being above it. Formal NGP (Non-Graded Pass) will be decided at a viva assessment / exhibition event at the end of the course.

CLO Pass
Identify and describe AI and its responsible uses.
  • Accurately identify a wide range of AI technologies such as machine learning, neural networks, and large language models. Describe at least one use-case for each type of AI.
  • Provide detailed examples of responsible uses in at least one area of industry. Describe the responsible use with general statements in areas such as ethical or legal considerations, accountability, and societal impacts.
Reflect upon, and discuss the implications of AI on individuals, cultures, industries and the environment.
  • Reflect on a diverse range of implications on individuals, cultures, industries and the environment.
  • Demonstrate a knowledge of both positive and negative outcomes.
  • Discuss implications, drawing on detailed examples from multiple sectors including education.
Explain the need for ethical, legal and regulatory frameworks to govern AI.
  • Explain the purpose and the necessity of having ethical, legal, and regulatory frameworks.
  • Describe the effectiveness and limitations of these frameworks.
  • Reference at least one real-world example of a relevant regulatory framework and its application.
Critically analyse the use of AI relevant to their life, education and career.
  • Identify and describe key AI technologies used in personal, educational, and professional contexts.
  • Analyse the benefits and limitations of AI in these contexts, considering factors such as efficiency, bias, and accessibility, recognising potential biases and unintended consequences.
  • Reference and reflect on at least one real-world example of AI impacting life, education, or career.
Demonstrate ethical, reflective, and effective use of AI in practice.
  • Appropriately disclose AI use in their work, including model, prompts, and outputs.
  • Demonstrate appropriate use of AI tools in an academic or professional setting through demonstrated engagement with an AI based project, acknowledging their limitations.
  • Reflect on their own use of AI, identifying potential ethical and practical challenges.
  • Identify and address potential risks, including misuse, misinformation, and over-reliance on AI.
