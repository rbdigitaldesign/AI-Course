This week we look at Artificial Intelligence from a broad perspective.

  • What can it do?
  • How do we define it?
  • What tools can we use?

We will discuss assignments, how this course is graded and discuss how you will demonstrate learning in the course with an ePortfolio. 

Course Structure

This course will have 10 workshops, typically 2 hours in length to discuss issues, develop positions on tricky issues, understand multiple perspectives on AI use and develop skills in using AI. Do not think of these as tutorials. We will not be giving answers to questions but rather facilitating discussion so you can come up with your own perspectives on AI and its use. There will not be many tasks where you won't have to challenge your own preconceptions, so be brave!

A typical week in this course will look like this: 

  • An hour of preparatory work for each 2 hour workshop
  • You will be given space to work in groups and tutors will bring the class together to discuss key issues around the use of AI
  •  An example session might look like this 
    • Summary of weekly topic (10 mins) 
    • Class discussion and tutor feedback on weekly tasks (10 mins) 
    • Main class activity (40 minutes) 
    • Class discussion and tutor feedback of activity outcomes (20 minutes) 
    • Second class activity (30 minutes) including opportunity to add to ePortfolio 
    • Wrap up discussion, presage to next week and feedback on session (10 minutes)  
  • Delivery mode: Classes of 20-50 students either will be either face to face or online. Either mode will be provide you with meaningful challenges and the opportunity to engage with the content and other students if you ensure you contribute meaningfully each week. You will not be able to mix online and face to face classes.
