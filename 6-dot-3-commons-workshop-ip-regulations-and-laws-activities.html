During the workshop

Task #1: Case Study Discussion (30 minutes)

Having read one of the case studies above, think about what laws currently might apply to the situation. Consider:

  • How is the AI used?
  • Are there current laws which might apply? 
  • Which laws and how?
  • Who is affected by the laws?
  • Who benefits?
  • Whose behaviour might change?

Task #2: The Good, the Bad and the Ugly Revisited (30 minutes)

In last week's workshop, we looked at some uses of AI and the utopian and dystopian scenarios which could emerge (check your ePortfolio). Write down some of the legal frameworks (copyright, ownership etc.) which might apply to your scenario. Consider:

  • What laws exist that might help create a utopian or dystopian scenario?
  • What laws might we need going into the future to avert these disasters?
  • If you think a new law is needed, what will the consequences of that change be?

Task #3: AI Manifesto (50 minutes)

In everyday life, people often place stricter limits on their behaviour than the law requires. Laws generally set a minimum standard for acceptable conduct in society: they define what actions are illegal and punishable. However, individuals and communities frequently adopt additional constraints based on ethical beliefs, moral principles, or religious teachings. For example, a person might choose not to lie, exploit others, or consume certain foods even though these actions may be legal. These self-imposed rules reflect values about what is right, not merely what is permitted, and they often guide behaviour in situations where the law is silent.

With the help of AI, create a set of personal rules which you will use to guide your use of AI going forward and align with your own moral/ethical belief structures (you may need to think about what your values are). Reflect on how realistic your rules are and whether it is plausible to follow them going forward.
