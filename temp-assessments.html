The course will be functionally assessed as a single large ePortfolio of work comprising of multiple smaller components. The smaller components will be assessed against a rubric (but not with a numerical grade) and with feedback at several junctures in the course. The feedback will indicate whether the student is ‘on track’ to achieving the standard, or not, or being above it. We have flagged these as Formal Feedback points in the course. Formal NGP will be decided at a viva assessment at the end of the course.

3.2: Mapping and performance standard/success criteria 

Rubric (draft) 

CLO 

Pass 

Identify and describe AI and its responsible uses. 
  • Accurately identify a wide range of AI technologies such as machine learning, neural networks, and large language models. Describe at least one use-case for each type of AI.  
  • Provide detailed examples of responsible uses in at least one area of industry. Describing the responsible use with general statements in areas such as ethical or legal considerations, accountability, and societal impacts. 

Reflect upon, and discuss the implications of AI on individuals, cultures, industries and the environment. 
  • Reflects on a diverse range of implications on individuals, cultures, industries and the environment. 
  • Demonstrates a knowledge of both positive and negative outcomes.  
  • Discusses implications drawing on detailed examples from multiple sectors including education.  

Explain the need for ethical, legal and regulatory frameworks to govern AI. 
  • Explains the purpose and the necessity of having ethical, legal, and regulatory frameworks. 
  • Describes the effectiveness and limitations of these frameworks. 
  • References at least one real-world example of a relevant regulatory framework and its application. 

Critically analyse the use of AI relevant to their life, education and career.  
  • Identifies and describes key AI technologies used in personal, educational, and professional contexts. 
  • Analyse the benefits and limitations of AI in these contexts, considering factors such as efficiency, bias, and accessibility, recognising potential biases and unintended consequences. 
  • References and reflects on at least one real-world example of AI impacting life, education, or career. 

Demonstrate ethical, reflective, and effective use of AI in practice. 
  • Appropriately discloses AI use in their work, including model, prompts, and outputs. 
  • Demonstrates appropriate use of AI tools in an academic or professional setting through demonstrated engagement with an AI based project, acknowledging their limitations. 
  • Reflects on their own use of AI, identifying potential ethical and practical challenges. 
  • Identifies and addresses potential risks, including misuse, misinformation, and over-reliance on AI.
