Tutor Instructions

Overview

The main objectives of this workshop are:

#1: Cohort & Expectation building:

  • For these classes to work, there will need to be a good degree of cohort building. This means, introducing yourself to students, learning names, establishing groups where students will work together collaboratively (and openly).
  • These classes are interactive. You will need paper & pens to complete some of the activities.

#2: Assessment Expectations

  • Student work will be captured in the ePortfolio. They need to understand how to use the tool (technically), and how to engage with the tool (intellectually). The first should be fairly straightforward. The second... What is important is that students understand that this course isn't just another course. It is a NGP. Put the course learning outcomes and graduate attributes on full display here. For this course, they are:
    • Descriptive: Knowing stuff about AI
    • Critical Thinking: How does AI impact stuff, how should they use AI
    • Demonstrative: Using AI effectively (balancing numerous issues)
    • Challenging and interdisciplinary. There may not be any 'right' answers
  • To that end, they need to understand that the assessment of their ePortfolio is for them to demonstrate these things. We are promoting "creativity with constraints". The topic and content of the ePortfolio will be up to the student, but we do provide a template and a design sequence to guide them in their work. To achieve this you will need to:
    • Display and explain the design sequence
    • Provide ePortfolio template link
    • Guide students through the process of downloading and remixing the ePortfolio
  • Assessment 
    • The ePortfolio will have three 'check-in' points, marked by you (weeks 5, 7 & 9). Detailed written feedback is essential.
    • Checkpoints are not summative, they are representative of progress and 'trajectory'. Tick means you are on-track, cross means you need to lift your game.
    • You can show this assessment system to students manually.
    • The final assessment will be an aggregate of everything they do, evaluated against the CLOs and standards of the course.

Workshop Activities Guide

Task #1 & 2 (make some AI videos for these?)

Here is the game. Interview your peer and write notes about your partner. Use GenAI to write an introduction for your partner. You should start with some fairly benign examples. However, if the students are up for it, you can try some slightly more 'edgy' ones (you will need to read the room here). The goal is to have fun. Experiment and have fun.

An extended list of style suggestions:

  • A early-cinema 'NOIR' detective style introduction as if you were being described by the detective with a voice over in black and white
  • A David-Attenborough style documentary as if your partner were some kind of newly discovered alien
  • An introduction in the style of a 1990's ROM-COM meet-cute
  • Something from a Cyberpunk Dystopia
  • In the style of an 'Arrested Development' voiceover
  • A children's book narrator
  • A Superhero origin story
  • As if a commentator in an WWE (professional wrestling) describing a wrestler intro

An extended list of 'affect' suggestions:

  • From the perspective of being introduced by a 'self-help' Guru
  • From the perspective of being part of a conspiracy theorists belief structure
  • An overly verbose philosopher
  • As if it were an insecure internal monologue
  • Absurdly overconfident narrator
  • As a Wikipedia entry
  • As a job advertisement

Task #3: Group Discussion

A good way to promote group discussion is to first ask anyone if they'd like to share a particularly funny/interesting version. Encourage them to add this to their ePortfolio (it isn't one of the actual tasks, just give them a nudge).

The reading of introductions within group can act a defacto introduction to the rest of the group. The discussion should focus on the following:

  • What worked?
  • What didn't?
  • What kinds of prompts improved the desired result?
  • Long, short, keywords, language style, 'prompt-ception'?
  • Each group should be encouraged to demonstrate their best output.

Task #4: Story Creation

Story Creation. This should be fairly easy to demonstrate and set up. The stories will probably lose coherence after 100 or so words. You can interrupt the groups half way through the activity to explain that LLMs perform the same logic. One word generated at a time (including conversations etc).

Task #5: Final Discussion

General table discussion. Some extra question prompts for quiet tables:

  • Which tools do you like? What do they do?
  • What is the coolest thing you've done with AI? AI Art? AI Music? etc.

Wrap up

  • Remind students about their ePortfolio tasks (they are in the powerpoint presentation)
  • Students should be encouraged to 'do a bit more' for the ePortfolio. We are looking for the basic tasks, but students should be able to add anything they want that they think 'hits the brief'.
