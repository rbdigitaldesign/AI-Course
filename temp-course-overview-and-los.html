1.1 Course Name  

​​Responsible AI: Bridging Ethics, Education & Industry​ 

1.2 Course Summary 

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is revolutionizing the way we work, learn, and interact with technology. This course will introduce AI as the most influential technology to be introduced in the last 30 years and will encourage and support students in developing a comprehensive understanding of how AI is transforming learning, influencing changes in industry, and shaping societal dynamics. 

As AI becomes more ingrained in daily life, it raises fundamental questions about ethics, bias, privacy, and social responsibility. Through debates, discussions, and ethical case studies, students will critically analyze AI’s societal impact, evaluate ethical concerns, and explore solutions to ensure AI serves humanity responsibly. Students will reflect upon authentic case studies and presentations from high-profile speakers in inter-disciplinary workshops to understand the use of AI in the world and its impact, what ‘responsible use of AI’ means, and the ethical dilemmas of AI in decision-making and social justice. Students will document their progress weekly in a portfolio and choose AI projects based on their interests. They will be supported by regular feedback as they progress through the course, moving from demystifying AI, understanding the breadth and challenges in its use and the legal and ethical ramifications and finally put their understanding into practice in their project and summarise their learnings in a report built from their portfolio. 

SECTION 2: Course Aim and Learning Outcomes  

 

2.1 Course Aim  

Students will discover AI’s potential benefits and disadvantages and will explore the technical, ethical and legal issues related to AI, while developing expertise and understanding for how to responsibly apply AI in their studies, workplace and society. Students will work in an interdisciplinary, collaborative environment to demystify AI and develop meaningful expertise in its use through flexible and personalised assessment. The course will encourage collaboration, with a goal towards developing a culture of learning assisted by technology and multiple human perspectives.
