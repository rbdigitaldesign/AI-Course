Welcome to the course! Together, we’ll explore artificial intelligence (AI), arguably the most influential technology developed in the last 30 years. You’ve already encountered AI in everyday tools like Spotify playlists, generative AI tools, voice assistants, navigation apps, and spam filters. But it also powers autonomous vehicles, deepfake videos, and even medical diagnoses.

Throughout this course, we’ll guide you in discovering both the exciting possibilities and the complex challenges AI brings from both technological insight and diverse human perspectives. We’ll unpack the technical, ethical, and legal issues surrounding AI, and you’ll build the skills to apply it responsibly in your studies, your future workplace, and society more broadly. You’ll work in a collaborative, interdisciplinary environment where we’ll demystify AI together, learning not just how AI works, but how it shapes the world around us. XXX

About this course

This course will have 10 workshops, typically 2 hours in length, to discuss issues, develop positions on tricky issues, understand multiple perspectives on AI use and develop skills in using AI. Do not think of these as tutorials. We will not be giving answers to questions but rather facilitating discussion so you can come up with your own perspectives on AI and its use. There will not be many tasks where you won't have to challenge your own preconceptions, so be brave!

We will discuss assignments, how this course is graded and discuss how you will demonstrate learning in the course with an ePortfolio.  

A typical week in this course will look like this: 

  • An hour of preparatory work for each 2-hour workshop
  • You will be given space to work in groups, and tutors will bring the class together to discuss key issues around the use of AI
  •  An example session might look like this:
    • Summary of weekly topic (10 mins)
    • Class discussion and tutor feedback on weekly tasks (10 mins)
    • Main class activity (40 minutes)
    • Class discussion and tutor feedback on activity outcomes (20 minutes)
    • Second class activity (30 minutes), including opportunity to add to ePortfolio
    • Wrap up discussion, presage to next week and feedback on session (10 minutes)  
  • Delivery mode: Classes of 20-50 students will be either face-to-face or online. Either mode will provide you with meaningful challenges and the opportunity to engage with the content and other students if you ensure you contribute meaningfully each week. You will not be able to mix online and face-to-face classes.
