This week we look at the rules and guidelines that currently exist with respect to artificial intelligence. We discuss the ethics of the development of AI tools, the harvesting of data to train them, how training occurs, what responsible use might look like and the laws currently in existence or being proposed around the world. We'll delve into topics like intellectual property and copyright and how AI interacts with (and even subverts) existing frameworks. We'll talk about data gathering, ownership and privacy.

This week we'll look AI from a legal perspective. Firstly, do we have any laws about AI? If not, what laws do exist that might apply? Are they sufficient or do they fall short? AI has been around for a while, how has it been dealt with in the past?

We'll also push past what exists already and look at where the boundaries of AI usage should be. What is ethical use of AI? What is unethical? What are we trying to protect? What are we trying to enable?
