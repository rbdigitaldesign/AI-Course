Draft Script for Intro Video

Your job is dead. Just letting you know. Actually, it is more likely that AI will be part of your job, than taking it away entirely. As of 2020, annual worldwide investment in Artificial Intelligence is in the hundreds of billions. To contextualise that number, world annual GDP (i.e., everything we do) is only ~100 trillion, so hundreds of billions of investment is a lot of money (0.1 % of everything). So, where is that money going and who is benefitting?

[Person in front of computer coding]

In the tech industry, AI is being used to write computer code, resulting in a ~15% productivity improvement depending on the project. This may change into the future, but people are still required to do a lot of the thinking.

Examples of complete automation are rare, though Amazon Go has stores with no staff where everything is handled through computer vision, weight sensors and cameras.

These two examples mark the way that AI can impact a workplace. Is it helping people to do their job (i.e., augmenting), or is it taking the job entirely (i.e., automating).

If you are in the first group (augmentation), is AI making your more valuable by letting you achieve more, or is it competing with you and so making you more replaceable? There is no easy answer here and it will depend entirely on what industry you are in and how you are employed.

This week we are going to look at numerous case studies to investigate workplaces and jobs where AI has shaken everything up.

By the end of this topic, you will know:

  • Describe how AI industry is using AI.
  • Describe the value AI can bring to industry and the inherent risks.
  • Identify processes and be able to define where the human must be in them to ensure reliable and valid outcomes for industry.
  • Apply frameworks for assessing AI readiness.

This topic is aligned to the following outcomes:

  • CLO4: Critically analyse the use of AI relevant to their life, education and career
  • CLO5: Demonstrate ethical, reflective, and effective use of AI in practice
