Task 2: Resume Creation
  50 min

In this task, you will try to create a professional looking resume using AI. First choose one or more generative text tools from:

  • The BIG list of Tools

Now, we are more interested in process than product, so the goal here is not just to make a resume, but to see how you can make a resume. You should consider:

  • Prompt content and style: Do different styles/instructions lead to different resumes
  • Accuracy: Did you get what you wanted? Can you fix it? How did you fix it?
  • Feel: Does the resume feel generic? Can you get some flavour into it?

Prompting Style Suggestion:

  • Ask for a resume template
  • Ask for a resume where you provide it the important information
  • Start with an actual resume and ask for suggestions (advice, no changes)
  • Start with an actual resume and ask for editing (i.e., write it again)
  • Ask for what the AI thinks it needs to make a good resume
  • Ask the AI what it thinks a good resume should include.

Task 3: Summarised Reading
  40 min

Read the provided article Wu and Zhang's 2016 study and write a short summary paragraph of what it is saying

Once you have written your short summary, use AI tools to summarise the article and consider the following.

  • How effective is the summary?
  • What works and what doesn’t?
  • What is left out of the summary? (content/style etc.)
  • How does it compare to your hand-written summary?
  • What does this mean for your studies?

Now that you've done read => summarise. Let's try the reverse. Do the following.

  • Choose article
  • Create an AI summary
  • Read the summary
  • Read the article

Consider some of the following:

  • Which method of summarise/read do you think will stick in your brain the longest?
