Project Overview

As part of your ePortfolio, each person will be expected to work on a large AI-driven project within a team of 5-8 students. The key focus of this activity is the following course learning outcome:

  • CLO5 - Demonstrate ethical, reflective, and effective use of AI in practice

Therefore, you should devise a project which ensures you hit this goal! What the project actually entails, is entirely up to you and this is an excellent opportunity to be creative.

Project Team

Your project team should consist of 5-8 members all from the same workshop group (we will have project-based activities at upcoming workshops). To ensure a diversity of views and skillsets, try to incorporate at least three different Colleges (i.e., students from different degree programs) into your team.

Poster

At the end of semester, we will be running a poster event. Student groups will present a poster like you might see at a conference. Now, for those of you new to conferences, a poster isn't just a static picture. Students are expected to be able to present to the poster. This often involves a ~5 minute mini-presentation, adapted to the listener. As such, what a poster contains can vary dramatically, depending on field, with some designed mostly to be eye-catching, highlighting the main findings, while others can be high detailed (to be read without an advocate). What form your poster takes will depend on your project, but here are some examples:

Format and Size

Your poster should be printed at A1 portrait size (594 × 841 mm) — the standard for academic conference posters. Set your document up at A1 from the start rather than working at A4 and scaling up, as resizing distorts fonts, images, and layouts. Export as a PDF before sending to print. If your tool gives you a resolution option, use 300 DPI to ensure the poster looks sharp when printed at full size.

Printing

  • Allow at least 2–3 business days before the event — do not leave printing to the day before
  • Local print shops near campus — several Adelaide CBD print shops offer large-format poster printing with student pricing. Hash Print (King William St) specialises in university poster printing and offers a student discount — check their site for current pricing and turnaround times. Snap Adelaide (Flinders St) also offers large-format printing; email them for a current student rate. Both accept PDF files.
  • Officeworks — a nationwide option that prints A1 colour posters on coated or gloss paper. Check current pricing at officeworks.com.au/print-copy before finalising your design. Accepts PDF; minimum 150 DPI.
  • Transport your poster rolled in a tube rather than folded to avoid creases affecting readability on the day
  • Lamination is optional but gives a more professional finish and protects the poster during handling

Accessibility and Readability

Your poster will be read from approximately 1–2 metres away. Apply the following minimum standards:

  • Title: at least 72pt
  • Section headings: at least 36pt
  • Body text: at least 24pt — text smaller than this will not be legible at standing distance

Additional design requirements:

  • Use a sans-serif font (such as Arial, Calibri, or Open Sans) for body text — serif fonts are harder to read at a distance
  • Ensure strong colour contrast between text and its background. Dark text on a light background, or light text on a dark background. Avoid grey-on-white, yellow-on-white, or other low-contrast combinations. A quick way to check: if you squint and the text disappears, the contrast is insufficient
  • Do not rely on colour alone to convey meaning — add labels, icons, or patterns so information is clear for colour-blind readers too
  • Leave sufficient white space — a poster packed edge-to-edge with content is harder to read and navigate than one with breathing room
  • Establish a clear visual hierarchy: your title and key finding should be immediately legible from 2 metres away before a reader steps closer for detail

Free Tools to Create Your Poster

You do not need expensive or specialist software. The following free tools all support A1 poster creation and require no prior design experience:

  • Canva — the most widely used option. Free tier available. Has pre-built A1 poster templates. Fully drag-and-drop.
  • Adobe Express — free with your student Adobe account. Similar to Canva with good templates and an intuitive interface.
  • Microsoft PowerPoint — free through your University Microsoft 365 account. Set a custom slide size to 59.4 cm × 84.1 cm via Design → Slide Size → Custom. Export as PDF when finished.
  • Google Slides — free with any Google account. Set custom dimensions via File → Page Setup → Custom. Export as PDF.
  • Figma — free for students. More powerful design tool with greater control over layout. Slightly steeper learning curve but widely used professionally.
  • Piktochart — free tier available. Particularly well suited to data-heavy or infographic-style posters.

A Note on AI and Your Poster

The poster is one part of this project where you are expected to do the creative and design work yourself. Using AI to generate your poster layout, write the text on your poster, or produce the visual structure misses the point of the exercise — the poster is how you communicate your work to a real audience, and that communication should reflect your own thinking, judgment, and effort. This is assessed accordingly.
