# AI Course Development Audit Rubric

This rubric provides a standardised framework for evaluating the quality of course materials at any stage of development. Apply it at each major milestone (draft, review, pre-launch, post-launch).

---

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **4** | Exemplary | Fully meets criterion; could serve as a model for other courses |
| **3** | Proficient | Meets criterion with only minor gaps; ready with light revision |
| **2** | Developing | Partially meets criterion; noticeable gaps requiring targeted revision |
| **1** | Beginning | Does not meet criterion; significant rework needed |
| **N/A** | Not Applicable | Criterion does not apply to this content type |

**Total possible score:** 48 (12 criteria × 4 points)

---

## Category 1: Learning Design & Constructive Alignment

> Do the objectives, activities, and assessments form a coherent, tightly-aligned system?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Learning objectives** | All objectives use precise, measurable action verbs mapped to appropriate Bloom's levels | Most objectives are measurable; minor verb ambiguity | Some objectives are vague or untestable | Objectives absent or purely aspirational ("understand", "appreciate") |
| **SMART alignment** | Objectives are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound | 4 of 5 SMART qualities present | 2–3 SMART qualities present | Objectives cannot be evaluated against SMART criteria |
| **Constructive alignment** | Every objective maps to at least one activity and one assessment item; gaps are documented | Alignment present for >90% of objectives | Alignment present for 50–90% of objectives | No explicit alignment between objectives, activities, and assessments |
| **Prerequisites** | Prerequisite knowledge and skills are explicitly stated with links to remediation resources | Prerequisites stated without remediation support | Prerequisites implied but not stated | No prerequisite information provided |

## Category 2: Instructional Design Framework

> Is a coherent design model applied consistently throughout the course?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Design model** | A recognised model (ADDIE, SAM, backward design) is documented and consistently applied | Design model evident in structure even if not named | Some design principles applied inconsistently | No discernible instructional design model |
| **Scaffolding** | Concepts progress from foundational to complex with explicit bridging; worked examples precede practice | Progression mostly logical; occasional gaps | Uneven difficulty progression; some concepts introduced without foundation | Content presented in arbitrary order |
| **Cognitive load management** | Content chunked into units of ≤15 min; dual coding used; extraneous load minimised | Most units appropriately chunked; minor overload risks | Some units are too dense; limited use of worked examples | Long, unbroken content blocks; high extraneous load |
| **Spaced repetition** | Key concepts revisited across modules through retrieval activities | Key concepts reviewed at module end | Occasional recap only | No revisiting of prior learning |

## Category 3: Tone & Teaching Voice

> Is the voice appropriate, consistent, inclusive, and engaging?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Consistency** | Voice, register, and person (e.g. "you" / "we") are consistent across all materials | Consistent in most materials; minor deviations | Noticeable shifts in voice between sections or authors | Inconsistent tone throughout; feels like multiple unrelated authors |
| **Audience appropriateness** | Register precisely calibrated to target audience; neither condescending nor inaccessible | Generally appropriate; occasional misjudgements | Frequently too technical or too simplified for the stated audience | Audience not considered; register inappropriate throughout |
| **Inclusive language** | Bias-free, gender-neutral; diverse examples; no exclusionary assumptions | Minor issues; overall inclusive | Some stereotypes, assumptions, or exclusionary language present | Significant bias or exclusionary language |
| **Jargon management** | Technical terms defined on first use; a glossary is provided or linked | Most terms defined; a few unexplained | Terms frequently used without definition | Heavy jargon with no definitions |
| **Motivational framing** | Consistent growth-mindset framing; "why this matters" hooks at each unit | Mostly motivational; some units lack relevance framing | Occasional motivation; mostly content-delivery tone | No motivational framing; purely expository |

## Category 4: Content Sequencing & Structure

> Is content ordered logically and structured for maximum learnability?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Logical progression** | Dependencies between topics honoured; no concept requires knowledge not yet taught | Minor sequencing issues; easily corrected | Several sequencing errors; learners may be confused | Content in arbitrary order with frequent forward dependencies |
| **Unit structure** | Every unit follows a clear Intro → Body → Summary arc; learning objectives stated at start | Most units structured; minor omissions | Inconsistent unit structure | Units lack clear structure |
| **Course map** | A visual or textual course map is available to learners from day one | Course map exists but is hard to find | Module list exists but no map or pathway | No course map or syllabus |
| **Content chunking** | Units are ≤15 min of active learning; long topics split with clear breaks | Most units appropriately sized; a few outliers | Several units exceed 20 min without natural breaks | No chunking; content presented in large undivided blocks |

## Category 5: Content Accuracy & Currency

> Is the content factually correct, up-to-date, and credibly sourced?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Factual accuracy** | All factual claims verified against primary or peer-reviewed sources; sources cited | Minor inaccuracies; overall reliable | Some factual errors or unverified claims | Significant inaccuracies or unsupported claims |
| **Currency** | All references ≤18 months old or explicitly contextualised; AI-specific content reviewed against latest developments | Most references current; a few dated items noted | Several outdated references affecting content validity | Content relies heavily on outdated information |
| **Real-world examples** | Current, diverse, real-world examples and case studies integrated throughout | Examples present but limited in diversity or recency | Few examples; mostly abstract explanations | No real-world examples |
| **Balanced perspectives** | Multiple viewpoints presented where relevant; limitations and critiques acknowledged | Generally balanced; minor omissions | One-sided framing on some topics | Significant bias; counterarguments ignored |

## Category 6: Assessment Design

> Are assessments valid, reliable, and aligned with learning outcomes?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Formative / summative balance** | Clear mix: low-stakes checks (quizzes, reflections) and evaluated assessments; feedback loops between both | Both types present; feedback loop limited | Either formative or summative dominant; limited variety | Only one type of assessment; no feedback loop |
| **Rubrics** | Detailed rubrics with performance descriptors for every graded task; shared with learners before submission | Rubrics present for most tasks; some vague descriptors | Rubrics exist but are incomplete or not shared in advance | No rubrics; grading criteria unclear |
| **Authentic assessment** | Tasks mirror real-world problems; learners produce artefacts with genuine utility | Tasks mostly authentic; some are purely academic exercises | Mix of authentic and decontextualised tasks | All tasks are decontextualised (fill-in, recall only) |
| **Academic integrity** | Clear guidance on AI-use policy, plagiarism, and collaboration; built into task design | Guidance present but brief | Guidance mentioned in one place only | No academic integrity guidance |
| **Feedback & timelines** | Submission windows, feedback timelines, and revision opportunities clearly stated | Most logistics clear; minor gaps | Some logistics missing | Assessment logistics unclear or absent |

## Category 7: Learner Engagement & Interactivity

> Does the course actively involve learners beyond passive consumption?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Active learning** | Every unit includes at least one activity requiring learner output (practice problem, quiz, reflection, code exercise) | Most units have active learning; a few passive | Active learning in <50% of units | Entirely passive; no learner output required |
| **Multimedia purposefulness** | Each media element (video, diagram, animation) serves a clear instructional function; no decorative media | Media mostly purposeful; minor decorative use | Some media is decorative or redundant | Media used arbitrarily; does not enhance learning |
| **Relevance hooks** | Every module opens with a "why this matters" prompt tied to learner goals or real-world impact | Most modules have relevance framing | Occasional relevance hooks; many modules dive straight into content | No relevance framing; purely content-delivery |
| **Variety** | At least 3 distinct activity types per module (e.g. reading, coding, reflection, discussion) | 2 activity types per module | Predominantly one activity type | Single activity type throughout |

## Category 8: Accessibility & Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

> Can all learners access and benefit from the content?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Multiple representations** | Content available in at least 2 modalities (text + audio, text + visual) with equivalent information | 2 modalities for most content; minor gaps | 1 primary modality with occasional alternatives | Single modality only |
| **Captions & transcripts** | All video and audio content has accurate captions and full transcripts | Captions present; transcripts missing or auto-generated without review | Captions on some content only | No captions or transcripts |
| **Alt text & image descriptions** | All images have descriptive alt text; complex diagrams have long descriptions | Most images have alt text; a few missing | Alt text present but often uninformative ("image1.png") | No alt text |
| **WCAG compliance** | HTML/web content meets WCAG 2.1 AA; tested with automated and manual checks | Mostly compliant; minor issues | Several WCAG failures | No accessibility consideration for web content |
| **Pacing flexibility** | Content is self-paced or has flexible deadlines; no timed elements without accommodation guidance | Mostly flexible; minor pacing constraints | Some rigid pacing without accommodation options | Fixed pace with no flexibility |

## Category 9: Learner Support & Navigation

> Can learners find what they need and get help when stuck?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Instruction clarity** | Every activity has unambiguous instructions; success criteria are explicit | Most instructions clear; occasional ambiguity | Instructions often vague; learners may be unsure what to submit | Instructions absent or consistently unclear |
| **Navigation** | Consistent navigation; learners can always find: current location, next step, and help | Mostly consistent; minor navigation issues | Some inconsistent navigation patterns | Navigation inconsistent or confusing |
| **Help & FAQ** | Comprehensive FAQ and support pathways documented; response-time expectations set | Help resources exist but incomplete | A single support contact listed; no FAQ | No support information |
| **Pacing guidance** | Estimated time per unit and recommended weekly schedule provided | Time estimates provided; no weekly schedule | Total course time estimate only | No time or pacing guidance |

## Category 10: Technical Quality

> Are all technical elements functional, consistent, and maintainable?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Link & media integrity** | All links and embedded media verified functional; broken-link checking in CI/CD or release checklist | Minor broken links; remediated quickly | Several broken links or non-loading media | Widespread broken links or missing media |
| **Cross-platform compatibility** | Tested and functional on desktop, tablet, and mobile; major browsers covered | Functional on desktop; minor issues on mobile | Mobile experience significantly degraded | Desktop only; no mobile consideration |
| **Formatting consistency** | Uniform heading hierarchy, font, spacing, code blocks, and callout styles throughout | Generally consistent; minor deviations | Inconsistent formatting across modules | No formatting standards applied |
| **File & naming conventions** | Consistent, descriptive file naming; no spaces; version indicated where relevant | Mostly consistent; minor violations | Mixed conventions; some files difficult to locate | Arbitrary naming; files hard to locate or version |

## Category 11: Course-Level Coherence

> Does the course feel like a unified learning experience, not a collection of disconnected modules?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Narrative throughline** | An overarching story, problem, or theme ties all modules together; referenced throughout | Throughline present; not always reinforced | Loose thematic connection; modules feel somewhat independent | No connecting narrative; modules feel disconnected |
| **Consistent terminology** | A glossary is maintained; terms used consistently across all modules | Terms mostly consistent; a few contradictions | Terminology inconsistent; same concept named differently in different modules | Inconsistent and contradictory terminology throughout |
| **Course framing** | A strong course introduction sets context, audience, and journey; a conclusion synthesises learning and points forward | Introduction and conclusion present; could be stronger | Either introduction or conclusion missing | Neither a meaningful introduction nor conclusion |

**Section score: ___ / 12**

## Category 12: Metadata & Governance

> Is the course properly documented, versioned, and governed?

| Sub-criterion | 4 – Exemplary | 3 – Proficient | 2 – Developing | 1 – Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Course description** | Audience, prerequisites, duration, level, and learning outcomes documented in course description and repository README | Most metadata present; minor gaps | Partial metadata; some key fields missing | No course-level metadata |
| **Versioning & changelog** | Semantic versioning applied; CHANGELOG maintained with dated entries; breaking changes flagged | Versioning present; changelog incomplete | Version number tracked; no changelog | No versioning |
| **Authorship & credits** | All authors, SMEs, reviewers, and media sources credited | Authors credited; media sources incomplete | Primary author only; contributors uncredited | No credits |
| **License** | License clearly stated (e.g. CC BY 4.0, proprietary); reuse terms explicit | License stated; reuse terms unclear | License type mentioned without full terms | No license information |
