This week we're investigating the use of AI in academic contexts, assignments, research and report writing.

Tasks: Preparation

Task #1: Examining an Assessment

Find an assignment that you have already completed (it must be from a prior semester or high school - not part of a course you are doing now). It can be any kind of assessment, but preferably a report or other digital artefact. You will need to bring it to the workshop. 

 

Task #2: Evaluating Marking Bias in an Assessment

Try using AI to mark student assignments and reflect on how it can be biased simply through a description of the student.

  1. Go to https://equityinai.net/ai-bias-explorer/
  2. Select one of the common biases or create a custom bias.
  3. Try asking the AI to mark assignments based on different student descriptions (Our highest recorded difference was over 10 points, which is the equivalent of two students getting an HD and a C for the same assignment).
  4. Reflect on how AI use in marking may benefit some students more than others.

Task #3: Feedback (30 Minutes)

Using the past assignment (see preparation), select an AI tool suitable for evaluating the work and providing feedback. Use the AI tool to generate feedback and suggestions on what the AI thinks should be changed or improved. In doing so, consider the following things.

  • Is the feedback helpful?
  • Is the feedback vague/precise? (can you change this?)
  • Is the feedback correct/incorrect? (can you verify this?)
  • If you were to change your work based on the AI's advice, would that be acceptable?
  • How does getting this kind of feedback differ from getting 'spelling and grammar' advice from a tool like Grammarly AI?
  • Does disclosing the use of AI change your position?
  • How do you feel about the university using AI to mark your assignments?
