Tutor Instructions

Overview

This is the second Commons Workshop, so hopefully by now a lot of the logistical problems have been dealt with. It is only Week #2, so there may be some new students (late enrolments). Make sure to make them welcome (and be ready to answer questions).

Task #1: catch-up

Not all students will have prepared. This activity serves to integrate the prepared and non-prepared students.

  1. Ask students to honestly show their hand if they have not prepared for this workshop (there is no punishment nor repercussion to this: it will just help the tutor frame the learning better)
  2. Ask the students who have not prepared, to independently read 2.1 topic on Canvas [$WIKI_REFERENCE$/pages/g8ed1b5bb491832b02576c1988f2ba7ec?module_item_id=g1b89f8d1851247bbcb2c576c801011f6]
  3. For students who have prepped, work in pairs. Hand them this pack containing a job description and four résumés. Ask them to rank the résumés according to which is most suitable for the job, and to give at least two reasons for each ranking decision.
  4. Once the time is about right, bring all students back together. 

Task #2: H5P tool

This task involves using a specialised H5P tool. First demonstrate how to use it (try not to bias the students into thinking there are correct answers here). After students have used the tool, prompt a discussion about which aspects of students lives are impacted by AI.

Some conversation starters:

  • What did you have the most agreement on? Why do you think you all ended up at the same place?
  • Where did you have the most variation? Do you think that was because your lives are actually different?
  • Did anyone change their minds? If you did the spectrum again would you get the same answers?

Task #3: Creating resumés

Here the students are creating résumés. The goal is process, not outcome (although good process should lead to good outcomes).

  • Encourage students to work in pairs/groups, but remind them that they need to upload their own resume to their ePortfolio
  • Track their progress, let them experiment for a while, but give them some hints if they are really struggling to make progress

Some extra challenges:

  • Can you write a resume without giving it all the information?
  • What is the best way to give it the information?
  • If you leave out information... what assumptions is the AI making about 'good resumes' (useful for social science students etc).

 

Task #4: Summarised reading

Here the students are doing two things:

  • Working with 'summarisation'
  • Learning about GIGO (garbage-in-garbage-out) bias topics (meta)

I would suggest choosing a different news article and demonstrating the basic 'make a summary' command. If groups finish early, encourage them to try summary vs recall. Which method of reading/summarising helped them the most in 'remembering the content'.
