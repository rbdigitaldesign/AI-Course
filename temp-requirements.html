SECTION 4: Course Teaching and Learning Strategies/Pedagogical approaches 

This course utilises a group-based inquiry mode of learning. Students will come together regularly 3 hours per week to discuss the latest topic in the course. They will have prepared via a flipped approach through supplied online resources and engage with an interdisciplinary group around the current issues. They will use practical approaches by using AI to investigate industry problems thus providing an authentic learning environment. 

SECTION 5: Course Resources 

 

5.1: Facilities, equipment, software and hardware (University owned) 

  • AI carbon offset for the course  
  • Staff trained in AI, managing large groups and teaching 
  • Microsoft Office or equivalent  
  • Pebblepad or equivalent-eportfolio tool 
  • An AI tool free of charge for students (CoPilot?) 
  • In class technology to support student weekly table-based presentations but this requirement can be worked around 
  • Space to promote group work and provide opportunity for tutors to bring the class together 
  • 1-2 Auditorium based TEdX talks to launch the course and inspire new and creative thinking  
  • 10 workshops, typically 3 hours in length to discuss issues, develop positions on tricky issues, understand multiple perspectives on AI use and develop skills in using AI. Do not think of these as tutorials. An example session might look like this 
  • Introduction to weekly topic (10 mins) 
  • Class discussion and tutor feedback on weekly tasks (20 mins) 
  • Class activity (40-60 minutes) 
  • Class discussion and tutor feedback of activity outcomes (20 minutes) 
  • Break (15-20 minutes) 
  • Second activity (30-40 minutes) including opportunity to add to ePortfolio 
  • Wrap up discussion, presage to next week and feedback on session (10 minutes)  
  • Delivery mode: Classes either face to face or online. No hybrid mode  
  • Class size: 30-50 students typically. Larger classes would require more tutors per class
