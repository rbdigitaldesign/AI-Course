Before the workshop

Objective: To prepare for an in-class discussion and debate on how AI can be used as an intervention for a social issue, considering both its potential benefits and risks.

Preparation (Before Class):

  1. Choose a Social Issue:

    • Select a relevant social issue where AI can be applied. Examples include:
      • Education access
      • Modern-day slavery
      • Unemployment
      • Homelessness
      • Healthcare
      • Scientific discoveries
      • Cybersecurity

  2. Conduct Thorough Research:

    • Investigate current applications of AI related to your chosen social issue.
    • Explore potential future uses of AI in this context.
    • Identify both positive (utopian) and negative (dystopian) outcomes of AI use.

  3. Develop Example Scenarios:

    • Create two detailed scenarios:
      1. Utopian Scenario: Describe how AI could positively impact the social issue.
      2. Dystopian Scenario: Explain how AI could be misused or lead to negative consequences.
    • Include ethical considerations and real-world examples to support your scenarios.

    • Examples could include:

      • Healthcare:

        Utopian Scenario: AI assists in diagnosing diseases early and accurately, significantly improving patient outcomes.
        Dystopian Scenario: AI systems make errors or exhibit biases, leading to misdiagnoses and unequal treatment.

      • Education Access:

        Utopian Scenario: AI provides personalised learning experiences for students worldwide, bridging educational gaps.
        Dystopian Scenario: AI algorithms reinforce existing biases, potentially widening the education gap.

      • Film & TV Production:

        Utopian Scenario: AI lowers production costs and shortens time frames for movie/TV production, fostering creativity.
        Dystopian Scenario: AI appropriates work, replaces actors, or synthesises deceased actors without consent, raising ethical concerns.

      • Scientific Research:

        Utopian Scenario: AI accelerates scientific discoveries by analysing vast amounts of data and identifying patterns that humans might miss.
        Dystopian Scenario: AI-generated research results are manipulated or falsified, undermining scientific integrity.

  4. Gather Supporting Material:

    • Collect data, articles, case studies, or any relevant content to substantiate your scenarios.
    • Focus on providing a balanced perspective, highlighting both opportunities and risks.

During the workshop

1. Table discussions (25 mins) about the case studies, guided by reflective questions, e.g.

  • How do the benefits of AI presented in the case studies align with its potential risks? In what ways could the negative impacts be mitigated or turned into opportunities?
  • How does the use of AI in the case studies impact society at large, including cultural, economic, and environmental aspects? What long-term societal changes could result from the widespread adoption of AI as seen in this case study?
  • What ethical considerations should guide the development and implementation of AI in this context? How can stakeholders (e.g., policymakers, technologists, communities) work together to address these ethical dilemmas and practical challenges?
  • How has this case study influenced your perspective on AI's role in society? Can you identify any parallels between this case study and your personal or professional experiences with AI?What actions can you take to promote the ethical and responsible use of AI in your own life or future career?

 

2. Whole class discussion (25 mins) with a specific focus on the environmental footprint of AI use calculator. As a class, what is our impact? How has/will this course affected your use of AI (more/less/same)? Is there anything we can/could do to help offset these impacts?

 

3. Structured table debates (50 mins), groups are formed to argue for or against AI (drawing from Black Mirror pre-work) e.g.,

  • Tables are split into for AI and against AI and given time to prep and share pre-class research.
    • For Pro-AI Groups: How does AI contribute positively to the sectors you researched (the diversity of research in the group should help)? What are the most compelling examples of AI's benefits from the case studies and your prep activity? How can the risks associated with AI be mitigated effectively?
    • For Anti-AI Groups: What are the significant negative impacts of AI in this sectors? How do the examples from the case studies and research illustrate the potential dangers of AI? What ethical dilemmas arise from the use of AI, and why might they outweigh the benefits?
  • Debate Structure
    • Opening Statements: Each group presents their initial arguments.
    • Rebuttals: Groups respond to the opposing side's arguments.
    • General Discussion: General discussion where students can ask questions and provide additional insights.
    • Closing Statements: Each group summarises their key points and final thoughts.
  • Reflection and Takeaways
  • Whole Class Post-Debate Discussion: Facilitate a reflective conversation on what students learned through the debate process.
