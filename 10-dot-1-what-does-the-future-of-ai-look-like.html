Pushing Baby Birds out of the Nest

Whatever we write here about AI will probably be out-of-date by tomorrow, next week, or next year at the latest. The pace of change due to AI is bigger and faster than almost any other technological innovation humanity has ever encountered.

It is time now for you to tell us what the future of AI looks like — this will involve scouring the internet for a variety of sources.

Where to look

📚
Published Literature

Even outside tech, articles explore how AI is disrupting industry X, domain Y, and behaviour Z. Combine your AI knowledge with your domain knowledge.

📰
Tech News

News is written every day — some by AI — covering share prices, product launches, and policy shifts. A good pulse-check on where the industry is at.

🎙️
Expert Interviews

Five minutes on YouTube surfaces hundreds of perspectives — from genuine AI researchers (Hinton, LeCun) to complete grifters. Learning to tell the difference is part of the exercise.

A Philosophical Goal

We have discussed many topics within AI — the basic mechanics and the broader societal impacts:

Societal Impacts
  • Education
  • Research
  • Culture & Identity
  • Ownership & IP
  • The Workplace
  • Ethics

One thing we haven't discussed much is what is the goal of AI. Not the narrow goal of creating a robot that can think — but what drives our goal of producing the robot in the first place?

Is the goal to…
  • Make people's lives easier or better?
  • Make money?
  • Displace workers?
  • Create art — or just enable artists?
  • Create synthetic 'life'?
  • Enable social or governmental control?

The technology has raced ahead of the philosophical discussion about the technology.

When researching AI, think about your field — not just how AI will impact it, but where might AI take your field if it improves enough? Is that somewhere humanity wants to go? Try to consider more than just material changes, but the more far-reaching, society-altering ones.

Think beyond the material changes
  • What happens when every communication between two people is filtered through AI?
  • What happens when a person's job is just to feed information to an AI?

Speculative Fiction

One place to find some answers to these questions is in science fiction (see Resources for some suggested reading/watching/listening). These stories explore 'what would happen if', and AI is a common topic. Sometimes AI is just incidental, magical robots performing simple tasks. In others, it is a tool run amok. In others still, it is an exploration of consciousness and the human experience.
