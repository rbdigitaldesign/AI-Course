Before the workshop

Objective: To prepare for an in-class discussion and debate on how AI can be used as an intervention for a social issue, considering both its potential benefits and risks.

Preparation (Before Class)

Step 2: Conduct Thorough Research

  • Investigate current applications of AI related to your chosen social issue.
  • Explore potential future uses of AI in this context.
  • Identify both positive (utopian) and negative (dystopian) outcomes of AI use.

Step 3: Develop Example Scenarios

  • Create two detailed scenarios:
    1. Utopian Scenario: Describe how AI could positively impact the social issue.
    2. Dystopian Scenario: Explain how AI could be misused or lead to negative consequences.
  • Include ethical considerations and real-world examples to support your scenarios.

  • Examples could include:

Utopian Scenario Dystopian Scenario

Healthcare

AI assists in diagnosing diseases early and accurately, significantly improving patient outcomes.

AI systems make errors or exhibit biases, leading to misdiagnoses and unequal treatment.

Education Access

AI systems make errors or exhibit biases, leading to misdiagnoses and unequal treatment.

AI algorithms reinforce existing biases, potentially widening the education gap.

Film and TV Production

AI lowers production costs and shortens time frames for movie/TV production, fostering creativity.

AI appropriates work, replaces actors, or synthesises deceased actors without consent, raising ethical concerns.

Scientific Research

AI accelerates scientific discoveries by analysing vast amounts of data and identifying patterns that humans might miss.

AI-generated research results are manipulated or falsified, undermining scientific integrity.

 

Step 4: Gather Supporting Material

  • Collect data, articles, case studies, or any relevant content to substantiate your scenarios.
  • Focus on providing a balanced perspective, highlighting both opportunities and risks.

During the workshop

Task #1: Table discussion (25 minutes)

At your table, discuss the case studies you read as part of your preparation. You can use the following questions to prompt the discussion.

  • How do the benefits of AI presented in the case studies align with its potential risks? In what ways could the negative impacts be mitigated or turned into opportunities?
  • How does the use of AI in the case studies impact society at large, including cultural, economic, and environmental aspects? What long-term societal changes could result from the widespread adoption of AI as seen in this case study?
  • What ethical considerations should guide the development and implementation of AI in this context? How can stakeholders (e.g., policymakers, technologists, communities) work together to address these ethical dilemmas and practical challenges?
  • How has this case study influenced your perspective on AI's role in society? Can you identify any parallels between this case study and your personal or professional experiences with AI?What actions can you take to promote the ethical and responsible use of AI in your own life or future career?

Task #2: Whole class discussion (25 mins)

Bring the numbers you generated using the Carbon Calculator. Be prepared to discuss your calculations.

Task #3: Structured Debates (50 mins)

In this activity, you will form opposing sides on a debate at your table. You will be debating for and against AI. You should draw from your preparation work.

The Debate Structure

  • Opening Statements: Each group presents their initial arguments.
  • Rebuttals: Groups respond to the opposing side's arguments.
  • General Discussion: General discussion where students can ask questions and provide additional insights.
  • Closing Statements: Each group summarises their key points and final thoughts.
