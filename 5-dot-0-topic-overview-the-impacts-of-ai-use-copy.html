Topic introduction

Our focus on this topic is to consider the beneficiaries of AI through the lens of environmental impact, ethical considerations and social conundrums. The positive and negative impacts of AI are vast, and as technologies advance, they regularly change. We can't possibly cover the impacts of AI in this topic, so instead, we aim to set you up with the skills to identify the inherent tensions of AI use and reflect on it in your life, career, and education.

Presenter on screen:

"Welcome to this topic: The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly of AI. I'm excited to take you on a journey through the complex landscape of Artificial Intelligence. Today, we'll explore the dual nature of AI—its immense potential and its profound challenges."

The Good - Visuals of advancements in healthcare, education, and daily conveniences:

"Let's start with the good. Imagine a world where diseases are diagnosed with pinpoint accuracy, where personalised education caters to every student's unique needs, and where your daily life is made easier by smart assistants anticipating your every need.

AI has already made significant strides in these areas:

Healthcare: AI algorithms can analyse medical images faster and more accurately than humans, leading to early detection of diseases like cancer.
Education: Adaptive learning technologies tailor educational content to individual student needs, enhancing learning outcomes.
Convenience: From voice assistants like Siri and Alexa to recommendation systems on Netflix and Amazon, AI makes our lives more manageable and enjoyable.
These examples show how AI can be a powerful force for good."

The Bad - Visuals shift to data privacy breaches, job automation, and biased algorithms:

"But we don't live in a utopia. With great power comes great responsibility, and AI is no exception.

Consider these issues:

Data Privacy: AI systems often require vast amounts of data. This raises concerns about how our personal information is used and protected. There are enough data breaches and scandals in the news to know this is a problem.
Job Automation: While AI can increase efficiency, it also poses a threat to jobs across various sectors. What happens to factory workers replaced by AI-powered robots or customer service agents replaced by chatbots?
Bias and Fairness: AI systems can perpetuate and even amplify societal biases. For instance, facial recognition software has been shown to have higher error rates in ethnically diverse settings

These examples highlight the darker side of AI, which we must address to ensure ethical development and deployment."

The Ugly - Visuals of dystopian scenarios, such as surveillance states and autonomous weapons:

"Finally, let's confront the ugly. These are the dystopian scenarios that keep ethicists and technologists up at night.

Imagine:

Surveillance States: Governments using AI for mass surveillance, infringing on civil liberties and human rights.
Autonomous Weapons: AI-controlled drones and robots making life-and-death decisions without human intervention.
Manipulation: Deepfake technology being used to spread misinformation and chaos.
These chilling possibilities remind us of the urgent need for robust ethical guidelines and regulations."

Need to know

By the end of this topic, you should be able to:

  • Evaluate how AI positively and negatively affects multiple sectors and industries.
  • Discuss detailed case studies that showcase both beneficial and harmful uses of AI in society.
  • Analyse the ethical dilemmas and practical challenges posed by AI.
  • Identify potential risks associated with your own use of AI in life, career, and education, and propose strategies to mitigate these risks.

This week you will do work towards mastery of CLO2: Reflect upon, and discuss implications of AI on individuals, cultures, industries and the environment and CLO4: Critically analyse the use of AI relevant to your life, education and career
