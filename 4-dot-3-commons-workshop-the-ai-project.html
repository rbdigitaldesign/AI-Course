Overview

This week, we are getting the ball rolling on your group project.

Tasks: Preparation

Read the list of potential project ideas. Using this as a guide, come up with three different ideas about what you might like to create with generative AI. Keep in mind, you will be working with a group, so be prepared to accommodate the ideas of others.

Tasks: In-Class

Task #1: Brainstorming (15 minutes)

In your group, you will need to decide on what your project is going to be. One way to do this is to get as many ideas on to your table as possible. Nominate someone as scribe and get as many ideas down as you can (even silly or fun ones).

Task #2: Project Formation (10 minutes)

Now that you have all the ideas on the table, it is time to choose one and refine it. In making this decision, you may want to think about a few of the following things:

  • How is AI going to be used in the task?
  • Does the project support a variety of roles and responsibilities (i.e., is it actually a group project)?
  • How much effort (people-hours) will the project require?
  • [other things]

Task #3: Project Planning (40 minutes)

Now that you have the rough shape of your project in mind, it is now time to elaborate on the details. Some questions/issues you might want to consider:

  • What is the scope? Is it too ambitious? What will happen if it doesn't reach 100% completion?
  • What does 'complete' mean? How will you know when the project is finished?
  • What is the breakdown of tasks?
  • Who is responsible for what? (Note: if everyone is responsible, no-one is responsible)
  • What resources will you be using? Are they credible? How will you do your research ([library link])?
  • What skillsets will need developing? What can people already do and what will take time?

Keep in mind that you can use AI to answer or help with some of these questions.

Task #4: Class Feedback (10 minutes)

Share your idea with the class and take feedback

Task #5: Poster Preparation (15 minutes)

Each group will be required to create a poster [link] for their project (exhibition). You should spend this time thinking about:

  • What tools will you need for the poster?
  • How to summarise your project in this format?
